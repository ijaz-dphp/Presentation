# PART A: The GIE & The Need for Change

- **2003 Turning Point**: HR Leader Randy MacDonald & CEO Sam Palmisano recognize globalization is fundamentally reshaping business operations.
- **The Concept**: Shift from a multinational model (classic country-by-country operations) to the **Globally Integrated Enterprise (GIE)**.

> *"Doing the right tasks, with the right skills, in the right places."*

# Importance of Human Capital

- **Knowledge-Driven Economy**: IBM's competitive advantage shifted away from hardware products towards employee knowledge, services, and expertise.
- **Scale of Operations**: IBM employed ~350,000 workers worldwide in 2003.
- **The Problem**: Existing workforce systems were country-centric, inflexible, and lacked global visibility.

---

# Seven Keys to Success in a GIE

To succeed in a globally integrated world, IBM identified 7 workforce priorities:

1. **Demographics**: Understand workforce capabilities and demographics.
2. **Predictive Capability**: Forecast labor supply and demand.
3. **Social Networks**: Use collaboration platforms to share knowledge.
4. **Location-Agnostic**: Enable work from any location.
5. **Collaboration**: Promote work across boundaries.
6. **Skills Development**: Rapidly upskill employees.
7. **Performance Management**: Evaluate performance & provide feedback.

**Core Principle:** Right Person + Right Skills + Right Time + Right Place + Right Cost.

# IBM's Transformation Journey

- **1911**: Founded. Watson Sr. builds a strong culture of loyalty, values, and innovation.
- **Growth Era**: Pioneered IBM 360 computer, FORTRAN, and disk storage.
- **1990s Crisis**: Profits dropped from **$6B (1990)** to **-$2.8B (1991)**. Suffered from bureaucracy and slow decision-making.
- **Turnaround (Lou Gerstner)**: Refocused on cost-cutting, e-business, and integrated customer-centric solutions.
- **GIE Era (Sam Palmisano - 2002)**: Launched "One IBM" strategy and the GIE.

# The Core Limitation: "No Common Language"

- **No Shared Taxonomy**: Different countries and business units described identical roles and skills differently.
- **Consequences**:
  - Impossible to compare skills across regions.
  - Expatriate assignments were slow and expensive.
  - Over-reliance on personal networks to staff teams.
- **Conclusion**: IBM urgently needed a standardized global skills taxonomy and a competency-based workforce management model.

# The Central Piece: Expertise Taxonomy

A standardized, enterprise-wide framework for describing work and talent globally.

### The 5-Level Hierarchy
1. **Primary Job Category (PJC)** — Broad category of work (e.g., *IT Specialist*)
2. **Secondary Job Category (SJC)** — Specific function (e.g., *Technical Services*)
3. **Job Role (JR)** — Cluster of responsibilities (e.g., *Client Infrastructure Specialist*)
4. **Job Role Skill Set (JRSS)** — Specific specialization (e.g., *Asset Reuse*)
5. **Skills** — Concrete knowledge/ability (e.g., *Implement Asset Management Services*)

---

# Benefits of the Expertise Taxonomy

- **For Employees**:
  - Clearer career path visibility.
  - Self-assessment against future roles.
  - Personal learning recommendations to bridge gaps.
- **For Managers**:
  - Better project resource planning.
  - Faster talent deployment (filling vacancies).
- **For IBM as a Whole**:
  - Maximized billable utilization.
  - Minimized regional talent shortages.
  - Full organizational transparency of skills.

---

# Redefining HR & Governance

- **Key Principle**: HR should not define the work; the people doing the work are the real experts.
- **HR's Role**: Changed from *creator* of definitions to *facilitator and governance manager*.
- **Governance System**:
  - **Taxonomy Stewards**: Monitored validity, reviewed modifications, and ensured business relevance.
  - **Governance Board**: Approved updates, ensured global consistency, and kept taxonomy aligned with industry standards.
- **Simplification**: Reduced job roles from **650 (2005)** to **fewer than 300 (2009)**.

---

# Global Talent Marketplace & Learning

- **Single Talent Ecosystem**: Integrated employees, contractors, and applicants.
- **Automated Matching**: Programmatically matched project requirements with available skills.
- **Learning & Development Integration**:
  - Linked over **40,000 learning assets** directly to specific skills in the taxonomy.
  - Allowed "just-in-time" learning targeted at resolving individual skill gaps.

---

# Financial & Business Results (2003 - 2008)

- **Billable Utilization**: Increased by <span class="highlight">9 percentage points</span>.
- **Fill Rates**: Exceeded **90%** globally.
- **Investment vs Return**:
  - **Total WMI Investment**: $230 Million (over 5 years).
  - **Total Business Benefits**: <span class="highlight">$1.5 Billion</span>.
  - **Direct Bottom-Line Savings**: **$453 Million**.
- **Contractor Optimization**: Achieved lower contractor costs and better selection through skill transparency.

# Redesigning HR for GIE Support

- **Old HR**: Siloed departments (Compensation, Learning, Diversity, Talent). Slow and inflexible.
- **The Globally Integrated Support Functions (GISF) Vision**:
  - Support functions (HR, Finance, IT) must run as globally integrated systems, not isolated country departments.
  - Reengineered processes to eliminate duplication, automate routine tasks, and lower costs.

# The Dual-Hatting Model

The most critical organizational innovation in IBM's HR restructuring:

```
                  ┌─────────────────────────────────┐
                  │        Senior HR Leader         │
                  └────────────────┬────────────────┘
                                   │
         ┌─────────────────────────┴─────────────────────────┐
         ▼                                                   ▼
┌──────────────────┐                               ┌──────────────────┐
│  Functional Role  │                               │ Business Partner │
│ (e.g. Head of    │                               │  (e.g. Supports  │
│   Recruitment)   │                               │ Global Services) │
└──────────────────┘                               └──────────────────┘
```
**Impact:** Deeply aligned HR process designs directly with real-world business unit needs while slashing bureaucratic overhead.

# Strategic Challenges & Dilemmas

- **Future HR Competencies**: The need for "hybrid professionals" who possess deep business understanding, data analytics, and HR expertise.
- **Manager as Talent Strategist**: Business leaders must take ownership of learning, motivation, and workforce planning.
- **Commercialization**: IBM recognized it could sell its WMI expertise and system to external clients as a consulting product.
- **Data vs. Human Values**:
  - *The Risk*: Becoming *too* data-driven and losing the "soft" human elements (trust, commitment, culture).
  - *The Challenge*: Balancing hard analytics with human empathy.

---

# Key Lessons Learned

1. **HR as Strategic Partner**: HR must shift from administrative overhead to a core strategic business enabler.
2. **Talent is a Supply Chain**: Skills must be managed with supply-chain discipline, yet with deep respect for human values.
3. **Common Language is Essential**: Standardizing roles and skills is a non-negotiable prerequisite for global mobility.
4. **Data Analytics + Empathy**: Successful modern organizations combine predictive modeling with a strong cultural foundation.
